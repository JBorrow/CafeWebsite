Journey to the Centre for Life


02 March 2013

As a response to Catherine and Rebecca's wonderful idea at a past Café, Café Scientifique and the Natural Sciences Society are instead of a Café hosting a 'Class Trip' to Newcastle titled 'The Journey to the Centre for Life' which actually coincides with our first anniversary, with the first Café Scientifique ever being a pilot in March.

Why the Centre for Life (or Science at Life) is so cool, from the website:

  • "Explore the Curiosity Zone - a 100% hands-on exhibition which encourages you to think creatively and act like a scientist.
  • Experience our stunning planetarium - the biggest in the North of England.
  • Join our inspiring Science Explainers doing demonstrations in the Science Theatre.
  • Have the ride of your life on our 4D Motion Ride (minimum height requirement: 1.2m/4ft).
  • Delve into our fabulous exhibitions including Human Life which explores where we came from and what the future could have in store for us."

I can assert that the planetarium is very impressive - it has an amazing video that delves into the human body and is impeccable for explaining biology concepts (aimed at children but still the clearest explanation I've seen!) and I personally loved the 'Remix Machine' when I went with Tim.

Hard Details: We will meet OUTSIDE ESTABLISHMENT BAR at 1:45pm sharp to walk with anyone who wants to to the station or alternatively you can meet us at station. We're getting the 2:15pm replacement bus service (but you must buy a train ticket!) which will appear in the car park of the train station.

Entry to the Centre for Life is £7.25 for students and £8.25 otherwise, but we can have group discount (10 for the price of 9) to make this lesser (by how much exactly we'll see by the numbers who come).

We think this will be a massively fun experience that everyone will find engaging, and it'll be a great chance to get to know people outside of Establishment bar. See you there!

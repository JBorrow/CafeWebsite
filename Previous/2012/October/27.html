The story of the double helix

   


27 October 2012

Chas Nelson from the Biology department will be talking this week about the double helix and the story of how understanding of the structure in DNA came about this week from both a physics and biology background and understanding. This should generate some ideas about the following discussion of women in science which will encourage scientists and non-scientists alike to consider issues in science from their own unique perspectives, as initiated by Chas and also Freya Varden, who has much experience in this area as a scientist and a female human being.

We hope to really engage people this week by first presenting people the tools they can use for discussion and then in the second half presenting an topic that can be thought of from multiple perspectives.

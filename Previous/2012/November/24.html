Science and the media

   


24 November 2012

We now have more choice than ever on how to consume our media and the mainstream news organisations are undergoing their most radical change in their 300 year history. Social media has added a massive amount of noise and accessibility to our already busy lives. How does science make the biggest impact it can in the current landscape and why should it bother?

This Café Scientifique will feature a talk and discussion as hosted by Institute of Physics journalist Naz Kinsella as he talks about how science and media (including social media) affect one another and how best to communicate scientific ideals in our brave new world. Please join us for this particularly unusual style Café that explores important questions that cannot be explored in science degrees and textbooks, where every perspective and contribution is valid and unique.
